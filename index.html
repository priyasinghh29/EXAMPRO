Competitive exams are assessments conducted to select candidates for admission to educational courses, recruitment for government or private sector jobs, or for various other purposes. These exams typically have a large number of candidates competing for a limited number of seats or positions.

  • Creating a study schedule and sticking to it.

  • Practicing previous years' question papers and mock tests.

  • Seeking guidance from teachers, mentors, or coaching institutes if needed.

  • Taking care of physical and mental health to stay focused and motivated.


  • Prioritizing questions based on difficulty level.

  • Setting a time limit for each section and sticking to it.

  • Avoiding spending too much time on difficult questions; move on and return to them later if time permits.

  • Utilizing reading time effectively to understand the instructions and plan the approach.

  • Avoiding guesswork and focusing on accuracy.


Failure in a competitive exam does not define your abilities. It's essential to stay positive and learn from the experience. Some steps you can take include:
  • Analyzing your performance to identify strengths and weaknesses.

  • Seeking feedback from mentors or teachers for improvement.

  • Considering retaking the exam after thorough preparation.

  • Exploring alternative career options or educational paths that align with your interests and strengths.
